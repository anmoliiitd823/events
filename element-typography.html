Acapellujah

Description of Event

Sing. Sing together. Sing your hearts out.
Blow our minds with your melody. Take our breath away with your swells of harmony. Own the airwaves as you let your voices become one. It is time to finally show what makes you a team.
Free yourself from the strings and the skins, you are the only instrument you need.
So come with your team, vocalize, harmonize, snap your fingers, tap your feet and show us the magic you can create together.

Acapellujah 18 is on!
We at IIIT Delhi would like to give artists a platform to fabricate music with the sounds generated only by the human body, be it vocalising, harmonising, tapping their feet, snapping their fingers or beat-boxing. Indian or western, language no bar, genre no bar. In A Cappella, the participants are members of an orchestra, in which they are the instrument, and they must use their voice and body in unison with those of others to stir the audience. There is music inside each one of us; the challenge is to let it out for the world to applaud.

Structure of Event

The teams should consist of 3 to 25 members each.
The event shall comprise of two rounds - an online preliminary round and an on-site round after the registrations for the event through an online form.
For the online round, participating teams have to record and send a video of their performance with all team members of their team clearly visible in it. The video may also be a recording of their performance in a recent competition (in or after 2017). The duration of the video must be at least 3 minutes and not more than 10 minutes.
Based on the results of the online round, 10 (number yet to be finalized) teams shall be selected for the final, on-site round. Each team shall be given 5 minutes of set-up time and 10 minutes of performance time. These times are independent and cannot be carried over, and exceeding them shall lead to penalties. The decision of the judges shall be final and binding.

Tentative Rules

1.Each team shall consist of 3-25 members.
2. 5 minutes of set-up time and 10 minutes of performance time will be allocated to each team (without any carry over), exceeding which might lead to deduction of points.
3.No back tracks are allowed.
4.Personal audio equipment is not allowed
5.No instruments are allowed
6.Cross-school or cross-college teams are allowed but information of the same must be given at the time of registering for the prelims.
